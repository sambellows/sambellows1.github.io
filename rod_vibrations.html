Vibrations of Rods

For rods, the general form is $A\sin k_n x + B\cos k_n x + C \sinh k_nx + D\cosh k_n x$ For a simply suppored rod at both ends $\Psi_n(x) = \sin k_nx$

Eigenfunctions for a simply supported rod at both ends.


For a free-free rod at both ends $\Psi_n(X) = \sin k_n x + \sinh k_nx - \frac{(\sin k_n L- \sinh k_n L)}{(\cos k_n L - \cosh k_n L)}(\cos k_n x + \cosh k_n x)$

Eigenfunctions for a vibrating string free at both ends.


For a rod clamped at both ends $\Psi_n(x) = \sin k_n x - \sinh k_nx - \frac{(\sin k_n L- \sinh k_n L)}{(\cos k_n L - \cosh k_n L)}(\cos k_n x - \cosh k_n x)$

Eigenfunctions for a vibrating rod clamped at both ends.


For a cantilever rod (clamped at one end and free on the other) $\Psi_n(x) = \sin k_n x - \sinh k_nx - \frac{(\sinh k_n L + \sin k_n L)}{(\cosh k_b L + \cos k_b L)}(\cos k_n x - \cosh k_n x)$

Eigenfunctions for a rod clamped at one end and free on the other.
